Steve Wittig - Drums

Steve started playing drums at age 7. He joined his first band at age 15 in the suburbs of Philly PA. From 1977 to 1982 he played originals and cover songs in Tulsa, OK. He released a 45 vinyl single with Rapid Fire Band, with 2 members who were to become part of Omen after moving to So Cal in 1983.

He helped form the Los Angeles metal band Omen. They toured parts of the US while releasing 4 albums and an EP. The band broke up in 1990, later to reform in the early 2000's. About that time they did several tours in different parts of the world. Steve finally decided to settle in the Sacramento area to play in tribute bands such as AC/DC, Pink Floyd, and ZZ Top.

He is currently the drummer for Walkin the Dawg Band and loves to play music, watch people smile, and dance.

Roy Chirigotis - Bass

Roy is a native of the Sacramento area. He learned to play bass in high school. He played in the high school band on the tuba and transistioned to bass when his friend started a band.

He has played in many local bands over the years. He loves classic rock and roll and sings lead and harmony.

Bryan Judd - Keyboards

Bryan is a long time native of Sacramento area. He has played in many bands thru high school and college.

He was a member of the world famous LDS Young Ambassadors Group while in college in Provo, UT. They toured the World and US for several months. He regularily plays organ and piano in the LDS Church today.

He is currently the keyboard player for Walkin the Dawg Band. A true fan of Classic Rock and Roll, he loves to play music!

Steve Wood - Guitar

Steve started playing guitar at age 7. He got interested in guitar after listening to "Secret Agent Man" on the radio. His first guitar was an acoustic his Mom bought that cost 10 green-stamp books! His Dad bought him a Fender Stratocaster when he was a junior in high school.

He formed the band Rampage after high school in Idaho Falls, ID. After he graduated college in 1976, Rampage became LightYear and quickly became the top band in Eastern Idaho. LightYear was signed to London Records and went on tour for a year and a half around the western US. They released a single 45 called "Forever With You" that landed on The Billboard Top 100.

After moving to Sacramento in 1985, he joined a band called Slick and The Sliders in Davis. He left that band in 1996 and formed Walkin The Dawg Band. The band has been playing at venues in the area for 22 years. A fan of Classic Rock and Roll, Steve plays lead guitar, sings lead and harmony vocals.
